FIRST
FIRST stands for For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology. Founded by Dean Kamen, it is considered by him as his greatest creation. FIRST is an organization that promotes STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics) to students all around the world by inspiring them to design and build robots for a competition. FIRST hosts four competitions: Jr. FLL, FLL, FTC, and FRC.

FLL
       FIRST Lego League, or FLL, is a middle school level competition
FTC
       FIRST Tech Challenge, or FTC, is a high school level competition originally designed to help bridge FIRST Lego League and FIRST Robotics Competition, allowing students to go from Legos to high level robotics more smoothly. But now, FTC has become its own competition. Building one and a half cube feet of robot is not as easy as it sounds. In September, teams all around the world pick up the game manual and start working. Given a set list of parts, teams are limited to using only certain materials for their robot, making the game harder and more challenging. Fitting mechanisms into the 18' cube isn't easy, but this is what thousands and thousands of high school teams do from September to March, in order to compete in one of the best competitions in the world, FIRST Tech Challenge.
FRC
       FIRST Robotics Competition, or FRC, is the first of the four FIRST competitions. It is a high school level competition, with the original purpose of inspiring a new generation of engineers and makers to build a better future. Every year, the game is released in January on Kickoff Day. Kickoff Day begins the 6 week journey that every FRC team must take, starting with a kit of parts, and ending with a competition capable robot.
